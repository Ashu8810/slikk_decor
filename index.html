Ideal Customer Profile
for Home Decor

Slikk's home decor buyer isn't a homemaker — she's a space-curator. She decorates for the 'gram, for the vibe, for herself. The ICP maps across three distinct personas, all concentrated in HSR Layout, Koramangala, and Indiranagar — micromarkets where the median tenant is 22–28, renting a 1–2 BHK or PG flat, earning ₹6–20L/year at a tech firm or startup.

The Aesthetic Renter

Primary ICP · 55%

Female, 21–26. Lives in HSR/Koramangala, works at a startup or MNC. Saves Pinterest boards of "aesthetic room setups." Makes small, frequent home-refresh purchases — a new lamp before a housewarming, fairy lights before Diwali, a quirky mug for her WFH desk.

  • Orders on impulse, often late evening after scrolling reels
  • Buys by "vibe" not by specification or brand
  • Shares hauls on Instagram Stories — organic WOM
  • Avg session: ₹600–1,800 (2–3 items)

The New-Nester

Secondary ICP · 30%

Gender-neutral, 24–30. Recently moved into a new flat. Urgently needs to make it feel "home." Doesn't want to wait 5 days for Amazon. Wants curated, ready-to-display pieces delivered now to set the room up this weekend.

  • High AOV: ₹2,000–5,000 in first month of moving
  • Occasion-driven: move-in, home makeover
  • Values Try & Buy — unsure about colors and scale
  • Highest LTV potential — will become a repeat buyer

The Host / Event Buyer

Tertiary ICP · 15%

Age 22–29. Planning a house party, Diwali get-together, or birthday. Needs festive candles, throw pillows, fairy lights in the next 60 minutes. Pure impulse + extreme urgency — nobody else can deliver a scented candle set for tonight's dinner party.

  • Highly seasonal (festivals, Friday–Saturday nights)
  • Low SKU count, very high urgency
  • Massive word-of-mouth: "I got this in an hour!"

Market Research Backing

  • 89% of Indian Gen Z say social media will be their top shopping channel by 2030 (DHL E-commerce Trends 2025)
  • India's online home décor market is growing at 10.9% CAGR through 2029, adding $4.39B — Gen Z is reshaping this as they prioritize aesthetic living spaces (Euromonitor 2025)
  • 41% of Indian consumers shop online 2–3× per week; convenience is the #1 driver (RedSeer 2025)
  • Myntra saw 50% YoY growth in its Home & Living category — indicating fashion-first platforms can successfully cross-sell into home decor to the same young, urban TG
  • 43% of Gen Z home decor purchases are influenced by creator content on Instagram/Pinterest — aligning perfectly with Slikk's social-first brand identity
  • Impulse home decor buying peaks around: festivals (Diwali, Christmas), flat move-ins, weekends, and "treat myself" moments — all high-frequency behaviors for Slikk's TG

The Whitespace: Fast + Curated + Aesthetic (₹400–900 Band)

  • Speed × Curation Gap: Blinkit/Zepto are fast but stock generic items. Pepperfry/Myntra have curation but take days. Nobody is delivering a Pinterest-aesthetic scented candle set in 60 minutes in Bangalore.
  • ₹400–900 "Impulse-Aesthetic" Price Band: Dedicated decor platforms start at ₹1,000+. Quick commerce caps out at ~₹500 with zero aesthetic intent. This band is entirely Slikk's territory to own.
  • Try & Buy for Home Decor: Color mismatches and scale uncertainty are the #1 reason for home decor returns. No competitor offers try-before-you-buy for lamps, throws, or wall art.
  • Trend Speed Advantage: When "mushroom lamps" or "groovy ceramic pots" trend on Pinterest, nobody can get them to your door in 60 minutes. Slikk can match trend cycles in real-time.
  • D2C Brand Last-Mile: Nestasia, The Quirk Box, Soy & Cotton have no fast-delivery home in Bangalore. Slikk becomes their instant gratification channel — a win-win partnership.

Brands Relevant to Slikk TG (Benchmark Platforms)

Deliverable 04
Sourcing Strategy &
Price Matrix

Split into a Branded tier (D2C and established brands that carry social proof) and an Unbranded tier (manufacturer-direct or local-supplier sourcing where brand name is irrelevant to the buyer). The split varies by category — start branded-heavy, shift unbranded as PL matures.

Branded Segment (60% of launch SKUs)

For categories where brand signals trust or social proof. Onboard via consignment in Month 1 to minimize inventory risk, shift to wholesale direct-purchase in Month 2 once sell-through is proven.

  • Nestasia — ceramic figurines, mugs, trays
  • The Quirk Box — meme mugs, gifting sets
  • Bombay Dyeing / Spaces — bedsheets, throws
  • Mush / Tavera — premium throws, bath linen
  • Soy & Cotton / Scented Story — artisanal candles
  • Ugaoo / Treleaf — planters, plant accessories
  • Neon Buddha / Kolorobia — printed wall art

Unbranded Segment (40% of launch SKUs)

For items where aesthetic beats brand recognition. Source from Bangalore wholesale (Commercial Street, Chickpet) or platforms like IndiaMART/TradeIndia. These become Private Label candidates in M2–M3.

  • Fairy lights / LED string sets — local distributors
  • Tealight candle sets (bulk)
  • Cork / bamboo coaster sets
  • Photo display frames & clips
  • Cushion covers — solid and block-print
  • Basic ceramic / terracotta pots
  • Woven storage baskets

Sourcing Priority Sequencing

Launch first (Month 1): Non-fragile, compact, aesthetically Instagram-friendly items with proven sell-through on competitor platforms: candles, mugs, LED string lights, throw blankets, coasters, cushion covers.

Launch later (Month 2–3): Items requiring special handling (glass frames, ceramic vases), larger footprint (curtains), or more brand curation (premium bedding). Add after ops team builds muscle memory.

Deliverable 05
Private Label Strategy
✅

Verdict: Yes — Launch "Slikk Home" Private Label, Starting Month 2

Private label is a margin accelerator once category conviction is proven. Slikk's fashion brand already has strong equity among Bangalore Gen Z — extending that equity to a home sub-brand is natural. The playbook: position as "Slikk Home" — affordable-aesthetic, like H&M Home but in 60 minutes. Don't launch in Month 1; use M1 data to identify which categories pull before committing PL inventory.

Rationale for Private Label

  • Margin uplift: Branded products yield 35–45% gross margin. Private label in the same categories yields 55–70% at the same MRP — a 20–25pp improvement.
  • Exclusivity: Only Slikk can sell "Slikk Home" — exclusive inventory that no competitor can replicate, eliminating price comparison pressure.
  • Gen Z brand affinity: Gen Z is brand-curious but not brand-loyal in home decor. A Slikk-branded mug at ₹499 — if it looks good — sells as well as Nestasia at the same price.
  • Category identity: A cohesive Slikk Home aesthetic (earthy tones + clean lines + tongue-in-cheek copy) creates a recognizable sub-brand that drives repeat loyalty and app stickiness.
  • Timing discipline: Don't PL in Month 1. First prove demand and which products pull. Private labeling too early risks dead inventory with Slikk's name on it — a brand-damaging outcome.

Priority Private Label Products

Quirky Mugs

High repeat purchase. Brand name on a mug is irrelevant — design is everything. Slikk can create meme-worthy copy ("Deadline Mode ☕", "My villain era") that resonates with its tech-savvy TG.
COGS: ₹80–120 → MRP: ₹399–499 · Margin: ~65%

Coaster Sets

Zero brand loyalty exists in coasters. Buyers buy by aesthetic. Bulk-sourceable at very low COGS locally. High gifting velocity — often add-to-cart as an impulse item.
COGS: ₹60–90 → MRP: ₹299–399 · Margin: ~70%

Scented Candles

Fastest-growing home decor item for Gen Z. Soy wax with artisanal scent names ("Bengaluru Rain," "3AM Cram") and minimalist packaging — highly giftable and Instagrammable.
COGS: ₹120–180 → MRP: ₹449–599 · Margin: ~60%

Cushion Covers

Block-print and solid-aesthetic designs with Slikk's seasonal color palette. Perfect for "refresh" buying cycles. Low COGS, easy to store in dark stores, no fragility risk.
COGS: ₹80–130 → MRP: ₹349–499 · Margin: ~62%

Ceramic Planters

Plant parenthood trend is massive on Instagram. Minimalist white/terracotta planters are high-velocity. Low damage risk for small sizes, strong recurring purchase as users buy more plants.
COGS: ₹100–160 → MRP: ₹399–599 · Margin: ~58%

Desk Organizers

WFH culture = massive desk aesthetic demand. Bamboo/acacia wood sets have strong perceived value vs cost. Bridges home decor and everyday utility — repeat purchase driver.
COGS: ₹150–250 → MRP: ₹499–699 · Margin: ~55%
Pricing principle: Price PL products at parity with D2C brands (not below). The proposition is: "Same price as Nestasia, in your hands in 60 minutes." This defends margin while making the value prop irresistible. Offer a 15% launch discount for first 30 days to seed reviews and UGC.

In-App Strategy Notes

  • Homepage: "🏠 Home" category pill added as 3rd pill — warm terracotta color to differentiate from fashion. Full-width hero banner for first 2 weeks of launch.

  • "Trending in HSR": Location-personalized, FOMO-driven module. Updates weekly based on actual sell-through data. Hyper-relevant social proof.

Part C: Offline HSR Micromarket Campaign

"Glow Up Your Zone" — A 4-week hyperlocal activation designed to make HSR Layout residents discover, trial, and evangelize Slikk Home Decor through surprise, delight, and community.

Week 1–2: OOH Teaser

  • Auto Branding: Wrap 20–30 HSR autos with Slikk creative — a moody "before" room on one side, vivid glowed-up "after" on the other. Copy: "Your room called. It said 60 mins."
  • Café Table Tents: Place branded table cards at 15–20 HSR cafes (Third Wave, Matteo, Starbucks HSR). QR → app download. Copy: "Ordering a latte was easy. Your aesthetic home can be too."
  • Apartment Gate Stickers: A5 stickers at entrance boards of top 30 HSR apartment complexes. Copy: "Your neighbors already ordered. You're up."

Week 2–3: Activation Stunt

  • "The 60-Minute Flat Takeover": Partner with 2–3 HSR-based content creators. Slikk delivers a curated home decor haul unannounced (with prior consent). Film the authentic reaction. Creator posts a real haul reel — organic UGC gold.
  • HSR Weekend Market Pop-Up: 3-day aesthetics booth at the HSR Farmers/Sunday Market showcasing Slikk home decor. Touch-and-feel → QR scan to order → ₹100 off code valid 1 hour. Converts physical browsers to app users instantly.
  • QR Sticker Challenge: "Scan to see what your wall is missing" — QR stickers in restrooms of top HSR restaurants and co-working spaces. Links to Slikk's wall decor collection.

Week 3–4: Community Loop

  • HSR WhatsApp Community Drops: Work with RWA groups and popular neighborhood WhatsApp groups. Referral offer: "First 50 orders from your building = free delivery for 30 days." Peer trust drives conversion.
  • Apartment Lobby Display Rack: With RWA permission, place a small Slikk Home Decor rack in 5 top apartment lobby areas for 1 week. Residents browse, scan QR, get 60-min delivery without cold-starting the app.
  • "What HSR is Decorating" Story Series: Collect photo reviews from Week 2 deliveries and post as a weekly Instagram Story series. Hyperlocal social proof — "your neighbor has this" is the most powerful push.
